The booth built around touching real tissue.

A conversation that starts before the show, peaks at a booth designed to be felt, and resolves in a live reveal after everyone's flown home. Here's the concept, room by room.

01
The one-line concept

A booth is usually a place you walk past.
We're building one you have to put your hands inside.

MTF's real advantage is tactile — so we make touching the product the entire point. Around that centerpiece sits an engagement engine up front, a sensory hangout in the middle, and a set of invite-only subplots branching off the side. One opt-in channel threads it all together.

02
How the whole thing flows

One conversation, in three acts

→
Before
Assemble the room

A pre-show poll/debate. Scan a QR, answer one question, opt in by text. The audience is warm before the doors open.

→
During
The "Touch It" booth

Surgeons handle unlabeled materials and vote. Their reactions hit the big screens live. Food, hammock, darts keep them there.

After
The reveal

At a set time: what each material was, what the room chose, a prize for the best response, and a follow-up that opens a meeting.

The spine is a single opt-in channel — a WhatsApp group — that people join before, talk in during, and get the payoff in after.

The booth at a glance

Warm wood, honeycomb walls, and one clean table on the aisle line.

Hexagonal wooden shelving climbs the back and side walls — organic, premium, quietly echoing cellular structure. The touch-test table sits front and center. The big screens glow behind it. A flex-mesh hammock waits to one side. It reads as one designed space, not a trade-show kit.

04
The centerpiece

The "Touch It" blind test

A row of covered stations, each holding an unlabeled material — MTF tissue among decoys. Surgeons reach in, feel the difference with their hands, and vote on which they'd actually want to work with.

  • The hands decide. No claims, no brochure — just the tactile difference, discovered firsthand.
  • The vote is the opt-in. Cast it from the phone they joined the thread with.
  • The "why" becomes content. Their reasons flow straight to the screens behind them.
Blind touch test

05
Live response wall

The booth watches itself think

Two or three large screens show the group thread updating in real time — who voted for what, and the "why I chose it / what I felt" notes coming in off the floor.

  • A gravity well. People stop to watch the room's collective opinion form.
  • Social proof, live. Every post is a peer endorsement happening in public.
  • Localizable. Screens stay flexible — nothing baked in, nothing committed before it's verified.
The live screens

06
Dwell time

A place to actually stay

Not a sales perch — a genuine hangout. Good coffee and real bites are the simplest, most reliable way to make a tired attendee stop and linger. The longer they stay, the more the booth works.

  • Food — the dwell-time lever that never fails.
  • Syringe darts & a flex-mesh hammock — surprising, on-brand, photo-worthy.
  • Conversations, not pitches — the relationship starts while they're relaxed.
The hangout corner

07
Signature texture

Honeycomb wooden shelving

Hexagonal wooden cells across the booth walls — warm, natural, organic. They display samples, totes, and literature, and they're the visual signature that ties every angle of the booth into one space.

  • Biology, quietly. The honeycomb echoes cellular and tissue structure without saying a word.
  • Warmth against the white. Wood softens a clinical category.
  • Modular. Each cell stages a single object — easy to restyle per show.
Honeycomb shelving

08
A wink at the injection story

Syringe darts

A dartboard where the darts are styled as syringes — barrel, plunger, fine needle as the point. Light, competitive, and memorable, with a knowing nod to the Renuva injection workflow.

  • Play, not pressure. A game gets people to stay and compete.
  • On-brand by design. The prop tells the product story sideways.
  • A bracket = a reason to return to the booth later in the day.

The photo moment

The flex-mesh hammock

A hammock woven to look like surgical flex-mesh, slung in a clean wooden frame. A surprising, genuinely comfortable place to sit — and the booth's most photographed object, echoing the material story every time someone shares it.

  • Unexpected = memorable. Nobody forgets the booth with the hammock.
  • Earned reach. Every photo carries the mesh motif into a feed.
  • Comfort as a message — the material is something you'd want to rest in.

The takeaway

Mesh tote, not a pen

The giveaway is a well-designed mesh tote — useful, on-brand, and a walking billboard through the rest of the hall. It echoes the flex-mesh material story and earns its way home instead of into a bin.

  • Actually used — utility beats swag every time.
  • The motif travels — the mesh weave repeats the story all day.
  • Premium, unbranded-feeling — restraint reads as quality.

The whole system

Every zone, one footprint

The QR opt-in greets you at the aisle. The touch station anchors the center. The screen wall runs the back. The hangout fills the corner. Honeycomb shelving wraps the perimeter — and the off-site subplots branch off to the side on a thin connecting line.

This same node-and-branch logic drives the interactive planning canvas we build for the event — so every tactic has a clear place and can be moved, added, or edited as the plan evolves.
