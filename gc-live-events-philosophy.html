glassCanopy · Internal Philosophy

How we approach live events

Our point of view on trade shows, conferences, and booths — and why we build experiences instead of renting visibility.

glassCanopy
01
The default trade-show trap

Most booth spend buys visibility. Visibility is forgettable.

A logo on a lanyard, a bowl of pens, a looping video, a badge scan. Forty thousand dollars for a sponsorship tier that no one remembers on the flight home. The hall is loud, the surgeons are tired, and every booth is saying the same three things.

We think the money is better spent engineering moments than renting attention.

glassCanopy
02
Our core belief

People resist being entertained.
They can't resist being consulted.

A game asks people to play. A question asks for their expertise. The second one is almost impossible to walk past — especially for experts who fly to conferences precisely to learn what their peers think.

03
The shift

From buying attention to earning participation

The old model

  • Rent the biggest booth
  • Sponsor the lanyard
  • Broadcast at everyone
  • Scan badges, hope for leads
  • The event is a one-day blip

How we think

  • Engineer a memorable moment
  • Target the right people precisely
  • Consult, don't broadcast
  • Earn an opt-in worth more than a scan
  • The event is the middle of a conversation

04
Principle 01

One conversation. The event is the middle of it.

We design before, during, and after as a single continuous thread — not three disconnected campaigns. People arrive already carrying unfinished business, and they leave still holding it.

→
Before
"Hello."

A one-click commitment that earns an opt-in and captures a preference.

→
During
Live reveal

An irresistible data reveal, activity, or giveaway they have to be present for.

After
"Thinking of you."

Where their answer landed, circled back to the preference they gave.

05
Principle 02

The opt-in is the asset — not the badge scan.

A scan is a name. An opt-in is permission and a relationship. We use the engagement mechanic itself — a poll, a debate, a vote — as the front door: scan a QR, respond, opt in to a channel that carries the conversation across all three phases.

CHANNEL OPTION A

WhatsApp group

Highest engagement and real-time back-and-forth. A live thread the whole audience shares. Our recommendation.

CHANNEL OPTION B

SMS list

Lower friction to join, simpler compliance, but one-directional and less communal.

CHANNEL OPTION C

Email capture

The fallback. Familiar and easy, but the weakest signal and the slowest loop.

Principle 03

Engage every sense — plus the two nobody designs for.

Sight and sound are table stakes. The memorable booths add touch, taste, and smell — then layer in time and proximity, the two things the internet genuinely can't replicate.

TOUCH

The heavyweight. When the product is in your hands, the difference lands.

TASTE

Food is the simplest, most reliable way to make people stop and stay.

TIME

Countdowns and set reveal moments. A reason to be here, now.

PROXIMITY

Things that only happen here — or only off-site, in a smaller room.

07
Principle 04

The booth is the hub. The best moments branch off it.

A booth is a public stage. The relationship deepens in the smaller, quieter rooms — the invite-only dinner, the off-site hang, the thing that isn't about the product at all. We design the main stage and the subplots together.

THE HUB

The booth

Live engagement, the touch test, food, the big screens — the gravity well everyone passes through.

SUBPLOT

Invite-only dinner

A curated steak dinner for the highest-value targets. The real conversation happens here.

SUBPLOT

Off the clock

A movie, drinks, dancing. Not about the product — about being human together.

08
Principle 05

Precision beats lavishness.

We'd rather deliver one unforgettable moment to the right hundred surgeons than a forgettable impression to everyone. Creative plus targeting replaces sponsorship spend — the booth gets smaller and the results get bigger.

WHAT WE SPEND ON

The moment & the targeting

The tactile experience, the live reveal, the opt-in, the follow-up, and the ABM precision to get the right people into the room.

WHAT WE SKIP

The tier sheet

The $40K lanyard, the aisle sign, the blanket visibility nobody remembers. Routed around, not paid for.

How we plan it

We map the whole event as a system of nodes.

Every event becomes a timeline — past, present, future — with tactics that slot into each moment. Some run concurrently at the booth; others branch off as subplots. We can add, move, and edit each piece until the whole thing is clear and shared.

→
Past
Pre-event

Awareness, the opt-in, the commitment that sets the table.

→
Present
The event

The hub and its concurrent tactics, plus the off-site subplots.

Future
Post-event

The reveal, the giveaway, the personalized follow-up toward a meeting.

This is the model behind the interactive planning canvas we build for each event.
