# The MTF Biologics Booth — Concept, Build, and Image Prompts
### For the October trade show · glassCanopy

---

## The one-line concept

**A conversation that starts before the show, peaks at a booth built around touching real tissue, and resolves in a live reveal after everyone's gone home.** The booth is a hub: an engagement engine up front, a sensory hangout in the middle, and a set of off-site subplots branching off the side.

The spine that ties it all together is an **opt-in channel** (WhatsApp group recommended). People join it before the show, talk in it during, and get the payoff in it after.

---

## How the whole thing flows

**Before** — A pre-show poll or debate goes out. To respond, you scan a QR code, answer one question, and opt in by text to join the group thread. Now the audience is assembled and warm before the doors open.

**During** — At the booth, the centerpiece is the **"Touch It" blind touch test**: surgeons handle unlabeled materials and vote on which they'd want to work with. They post *why* they chose it — or what they felt — into the group thread. Those responses appear on the booth's big screens in real time. Around that sits the hangout: food, syringe-darts, a flex-mesh hammock, mesh totes to take home.

**After** — At a set time on the final day, the reveal: what each material actually was, the data on what the room chose, and a giveaway to whoever posted the best response. The conversation ends with a payoff, not a cold email.

## The booth, feature by feature

### 1. The "Touch It" blind touch test — the centerpiece
A clean station with several unlabeled material samples (MTF tissue among decoys — synthetic mesh, foam, animal-derived matrix, etc.). Surgeons handle each, then vote on which they'd actually want to work with. The vote is cast via the same phone channel they opted into. This is the heavyweight: MTF's real advantage is tactile, and this makes touching the product the entire point.

### 2. The big screens — live response wall
Two or three large vertical displays showing the group thread's responses scrolling and updating live: who voted for what, and the "why" and "what I felt" notes coming in from the floor. The booth becomes a place where the room can watch its own collective opinion form. Gravity well.

### 3. The opt-in QR moment
Signage with a single QR code and one provocative question. Scan → respond → opt in → you're in the thread. Tasteful, not busy — the question does the work. (The poll/debate IS the lead-capture mechanism.)

### 4. The hangout area
A genuine place to sit and stay, not a sales perch:
- **Food** — good coffee and real bites. The simplest, most reliable dwell-time lever.
- **Syringe darts** — a dartboard where the darts are styled as syringes (a wink at the Renuva injection story). Light, competitive, memorable.
- **Flex-mesh hammock** — a hammock made to look like surgical flex mesh. A surprising, on-brand place to actually relax. Conversation-starter and photo moment.

### 5. Honeycomb wooden shelving
The booth walls carry **hexagonal wooden shelves** in a honeycomb pattern — warm, natural, organic, a nod to regeneration and biology. They display samples, totes, and product literature, and they're the booth's signature visual texture. (Honeycomb also quietly echoes cellular/tissue structure.)

### 6. Mesh tote giveaways
The takeaway isn't a pen. It's a **mesh tote bag** — useful, on-brand (echoes the flex-mesh material story), and a walking billboard through the rest of the hall.

### 7. The off-site subplots
Branching off the main booth node, for the highest-value targets:
- **Invite-only steak dinner** — where the real relationship conversation happens.
- **Off the clock** — a movie, drinks, dancing. Deliberately not about the product.

## Image prompts — editorial cartoon style

> **Shared style block (paste into every prompt):**
> Editorial cartoon illustration, smart business-magazine op-ed art style — confident ink linework with subtle texture, generous negative space, gentle wit, not slapstick. Sophisticated and clean. Limited palette: cool glass-white background (#F6F9FA), teal (#2EAABB) and deep teal (#1E8A96) as lead accents, deep navy (#05162A) for line weight, warm natural wood tones for the honeycomb shelving, and a single warm orange (#E17F3D) reserved for ONE focal object per frame. Medical/scientific subject treated with restraint — never gory. No real logos, no baked-in readable text on signage or screens (keep them abstract). 3:2 landscape unless noted.

## Production notes
- **The honeycomb + wood + teal palette** is the booth's signature. Keep it consistent across every angle so the booth reads as one designed space.
- **The one-orange rule** carries from the existing image set — exactly one warm-orange focal object per frame guides the eye and ties this set to the earlier cards.
- **Screens and signage stay abstract** — no readable text baked in, so nothing commits MTF to a number or claim before it's verified, and you can localize later.
- **The off-site dinner (Prompt 9)** is the one place warm light is allowed to dominate, on purpose — it should feel like a different world from the show floor.
- These prompts produce *new* booth-specific assets to sit alongside the earlier editorial-cartoon set (touch station, food, hooks, etc.), which still apply.
